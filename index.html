Tackling food waste


The idea


Earlier this year, a friend of mine named Roman Short sent me a picture. He manages a Pagliacci Pizza branch in Bellevue, WA and the picture was of three large pizzas that they were throwing away because of lower than expected demand.

After conducting some research I quickly found that this was a widely experienced problem that results in millions of pounds of wasted food by restaurants every year. Additionally, I found that the existing solutions to this problem were generally complex, logistically cumbersome, and expensive. There has to be a better way . . .

The simple, sustainable solution


Over the next two months, I started prototyping a mobile application that connects restaurants with organizations and other consumers who want their free, leftover food. What follows documents my progress on the app and how it works in its current iteration.


1. Restaurants post food to their wall

Restaurants list leftover food publicly along with the quantity of people they expect to feed with it. and the pickup window for the food.


2. Users reserve food

Users can see all the restaurants near their location and reserve pickups of leftover food.

3. Restaurants facilitate pickups

Restaurants prepare food for safe and convenient pickups, and hand off food to user when they arrive.



Built with SwiftUI


This app was created using the newest, and more powerful framework from Apple, SwiftUI. As a result, the app is beautiful, powerful, and built for the future.
